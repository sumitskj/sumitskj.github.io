Why you can touch the board but can't dunk yet
You're at ~9 feet reach on a ~10 foot rim. You need roughly 4–6 more inches. At 6ft with a ~7'10" standing reach, a 25–28" vertical gets you there. Most people at your fitness level are jumping 18–22". This routine specifically targets the two things that give the fastest gains: reactive strength (how fast your muscle contracts on landing) and ankle/calf power (massively undertrained in gym work).

The arm swing secret
A proper double-arm swing adds 3–4 inches instantly — no training required. Arms start low at takeoff, swing aggressively upward as you leave the ground. Most people leave this on the table. Practice it separately against a wall — just jumping and swinging arms without worrying about height.
Progressive overload applied to jump training. Each week gets harder. Don't skip ahead.
Measure your jump every Sunday morning — same wall, same spot, same time. Chalk your fingers. Track the mark. You should see 1–2 inches per 2 weeks early on.
